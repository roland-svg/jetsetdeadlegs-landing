Ideas & Insights

Thoughts on empty legs, platform development, and building smarter logistics for private aviation. Written for travellers and operators who want to understand how this industry is changing.

What this blog is for

We're building an AI‑powered logistics platform for private aviation, specifically focused on empty legs and repositioning flights. This blog shares our thinking as we build, the problems we are solving, and the insights we are learning along the way.

Expect explainers, data insights, "building in public" updates, and honest reflections on what it takes to make empty legs more efficient and accessible.

Written for travellers and operators

Every post here should make sense to both sides of our marketplace. Whether you are a leisure traveller curious about empty legs, a business traveller looking for more efficient routing, or an AOC‑verified operator thinking about yield optimisation, this content is for you.

We believe the best platforms are transparent about how they work and why they make certain choices. That transparency benefits everyone.

What we write about

Platform development

How we are building AI pricing tools, verification systems, and marketplace mechanics that work for real aviation logistics.

Empty leg education

Explainers on how empty legs work, why they exist, and how smart travelers and operators can make the most of them.

Industry analysis

Data and insights on private aviation routes, pricing trends, and what we are seeing across key UK–EU corridors.

Building in public

Honest updates on our soft rollout, private beta progress, and what we are learning from real operators and travellers.

Where we are now

JetSet Dead Legs is live and moving from soft rollout into a focused private beta with selected AOC‑verified operators. We are finalising payment infrastructure through Stripe Connect and automated verification systems.

This blog will document that journey — the technical decisions, the market insights, and the lessons learned from operators and travellers as we prove that empty legs can be more efficient and accessible for everyone.
